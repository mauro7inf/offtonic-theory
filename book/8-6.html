So what should I know about composition?

You should have some understanding of both the material and the creative processes that go into composition, as well as the various musical elements over which you have control. So you should understand the differences between composing for sheet music, recordings, or performance, or other products like folk music, and be able to understand why certain elements matter for one but not for another, as well as what your role is in the creation process. And you should generally know what kind of thing you're creating, right? How much original work do you need to do? You should know about style and medium and genre, and you should be able to analyze stuff if you want to write in a particular genre.

You also ought to have a general sense of how one composes, and, to be honest, you should probably go and compose something right now. Doesn't have to be good. It may well be very good, but that's going to depend on how much music theory you already know, because we're still going to dive deep. You should know about the decisions a composer has to make, and, if you're good, you should know the right choices to make, too. That's the hardest part.

Finally, you should be familiar with the musical elements available and how you can manipulate them to create interesting music, including removing the big ones entirely so that the small ones can shine. And you should be able to recognize them when listening. This is a big one. You should never again find a piece of music boring because it has no beat. A piece of music may be boring, but find some other reason, please?
