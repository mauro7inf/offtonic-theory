Offtonic Scale Degree Trainer

This is the Offtonic Scale Degree Trainer. Click on it to start, and click again to stop. It will play a low tone, which should be interpreted as the tonic of a scale, and then another, higher note; your task is to quickly identify the scale degree, which could be any of 1, #1/b2, 2, #2/b3, 3, 4, #4/b5, 5, #5/b6, 6, #6/b7, and 7.

That's hard.

It shouldn't really be. The problem is that you get a fairly small amount of time to get context. Learn to use that time wisely, and you'll have a lot less trouble. With me, I can usually understand the scale degree before I can verbalize it; I may hear a 7 and know that it acts like a 7, but it will take me a few seconds to actually get the number. It just takes a bit of practice.

Is practicing this game worth it?

No? Maybe. If you have trouble identifying scale degrees in general, yes, it is. If you can already do it well, spending the effort to do it faster may just be a waste of your time. But some people spend time memorizing digits of Ï€. It's your time to waste!
